## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (plato-torch, plato-ensign)
- **holodeck** – Simulation/MUD environments (holodeck-rust)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/constraint solving (constraint-theory-core)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms
- **ensign** – LoRA adapter export from room experience
- **mirror-play** – I2I iterative training between PLATO systems
- **deadband-protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework

**Support Categories:**
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specs
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, service management
- **research** – Exploratory code, rabbit trails, experiments
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or historical repos
- **asset** – Brand assets, logos, visual identity
- **test** – Test suites, benchmarks, validation

**Status Tags:**
- **active** – Actively maintained, part of current fleet
- **stale** – No recent commits, but potentially useful
- **abandoned** – No commits >1 year, likely deprecated
- **experimental** – High-risk, proof-of-concept
- **core** – Essential to fleet operation
