## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet‑knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- `plato` – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, tile handling)
- `holodeck` – Simulation environments (MUD server, NPCs, sentiment‑aware)
- `fleet‑simulator` – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- `constraint‑theory` – Geometric/relational constraint solving (Rust core)
- `cudaclaw` – GPU‑resident agent runtime
- `flux‑runtime` – Self‑assembling bytecode VM
- `shell‑bootstrap` – Bootstrapping algorithms for external intelligence capture
- `mirror‑play` – I2I (image‑to‑image) LoRA training from dual PLATO sessions
- `deadband‑protocol` – P0/P1/P2 decision framework
- `zeroclaw‑bootcamp` – Agent training pipeline

**Support Categories:**
- `docs` – Documentation, memory, protocol definitions
- `tools` – Utilities, scripts, service management
- `research` – Exploratory code, rabbit‑trail investigations
- `archive` – Inactive, deprecated, or experimental repos
- `asset` – Brand assets, logos, imagery
- `example` – Demos, tutorials, reference implementations

**Status Tags:**
- `active` – Actively maintained, part of current fleet operations
- `stale` – No recent commits, but potentially useful
- `abandoned` – Unmaintained, likely obsolete
- `merge‑candidate` – Small repos that could be folded into a larger one
