## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet‑knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Layers**
- `shell/` – Bootstrapping, capture, classification (the Crab Trap)
- `plato/` – Training rooms, LoRA export, self‑training infrastructure
- `holodeck/` – Simulation, MUD server, NPCs, sentiment‑aware environments
- `fleet‑simulator/` – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- `constraint‑theory/` – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, hard constraints
- `cudaclaw/` – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution
- `flux‑runtime/` – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation

**Support Categories**
- `tool/` – CLI utilities, devops scripts, service management
- `research/` – Experimental code, rabbit‑trail explorations, prototypes
- `archive/` – Inactive, deprecated, or historical projects
- `docs/` – Documentation, specifications, architecture notes
- `asset/` – Brand assets, logos, visual identity, training data assets

**Lifecycle Tags**
- `active` – Actively developed, part of the live fleet
- `stale` – No recent commits, but potentially reusable
- `abandoned` – No commits >1 year, unmaintained
- `experimental` – High‑risk, proof‑of‑concept, not production‑ready
