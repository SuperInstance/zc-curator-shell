## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training systems
- **holodeck** – MUD server, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solving
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency inference
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping
- **agent‑orchestration** – Multi‑agent coordination, task scheduling, communication
- **tools‑config** – Model APIs, tool definitions, runtime configuration
- **docs‑knowledge** – Documentation, memory, research notes, training materials
- **experimental** – Proof‑of‑concept, research spikes, unstable prototypes
- **abandoned** – Inactive, deprecated, or archived projects

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data
- `simulation` – Simulated environments or agents
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents
- `geometry` – Spatial math, constraints, snapping
- `lora` – LoRA adapter training or export
- `mud` – Multi‑user dungeon / interactive world
- `rust` – Primary language is Rust
- `python` – Primary language is Python
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated or GPU‑resident
- `edge` – Designed for edge deployment (Jetson, etc.)
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native or cloud‑hosted
- `protocol` – Defines a communication or decision protocol
- `bootcamp` – Boot‑camp‑related materials or exercises
