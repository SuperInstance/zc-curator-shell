## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, self‑training algorithms, LoRA export.
- **holodeck** – Multi‑user dungeon (MUD) servers, NPC simulation, sentiment‑aware environments.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, constraint‑solving engines (Rust).
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, kernel‑level agent execution.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode virtual machine.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms for capturing external intelligence (crab‑trap).
- **mirror‑play** – Image‑to‑image (I2I) training loops between two PLATO systems.
- **deadband‑protocol** – Implementation of the P0/P1/P2 decision protocol.
- **agent‑runtime** – Lightweight agent execution environments (often Python).
- **research‑trail** – Exploratory code, experiments, one‑off proofs of concept.
- **tool‑integration** – Wrappers, adapters, CLI tools for external services (GitHub, Groq, etc.).
- **docs‑knowledge** – Documentation, memory files, architecture notes.
- **brand‑assets** – Logos, wordmarks, visual identity.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `rust` | `python` | `torch` – primary language/framework.
- `training` – generates or consumes training data.
- `simulation` – simulates agents or environments.
- `edge` – designed for edge deployment (Jetson, etc.).
- `cloud` – cloud‑native or lighthouse services.
- `prototype` – early stage, not yet integrated.
- `abandoned` – no recent activity, likely deprecated.
- `merge‑candidate` – could be folded into another repo.
