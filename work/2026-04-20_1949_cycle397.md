## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA export, self‑training infrastructure.
- **holodeck** – MUD servers, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, constraint solving, CAD‑like logic.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, kernel‑level execution.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, low‑level orchestration.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Capture‑classify‑score‑complicate algorithms for external intelligence.
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping.
- **docs‑tools** – Documentation generators, tile formatters, fleet‑wide tooling.
- **research‑rabbit‑trail** – Exploratory code, experiments, one‑off proofs.
- **archive‑abandoned** – Inactive, deprecated, or superseded projects.

**Tags (often combined):**
- `rust` / `python` / `cpp` – Primary language.
- `training` – Produces or consumes training tiles.
- `simulation` – Agent‑environment interaction.
- `real‑time` – Low‑latency, embedded, or GPU‑bound.
- `protocol` – Defines communication or decision standards.
- `tool` – CLI utility, formatter, or service script.
- `research` – Experimental, not yet integrated.
- `archive` – No longer maintained.
