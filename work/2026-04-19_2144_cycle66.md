## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet‑knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato‑system** – Training rooms, LoRA export, tile‑based learning infrastructure.
- **simulation‑engine** – Fleet, agent, or environment simulators for generating training data.
- **runtime‑engine** – Agent execution runtimes (GPU‑resident, VM, orchestration).
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric, logical, or behavioral constraint systems.
- **holodeck‑system** – Interactive narrative/MUD servers with NPCs.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Algorithms for capturing, classifying, and integrating external intelligence.
- **dev‑tooling** – Build, test, deployment, and monitoring tools for fleet development.
- **research‑trail** – Exploratory code, experiments, prototypes, or academic references.
- **brand‑assets** – Logos, wordmarks, style guides, and visual identity.
- **knowledge‑base** – Documentation, memory, protocol definitions, architectural specs.
- **archive‑inactive** – Deprecated, abandoned, or merged projects.

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `rust` – Implemented in Rust.
- `python` – Implemented in Python.
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch.
- `cuda` – GPU‑accelerated.
- `edge` – Targets edge/Jetson devices.
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native or cloud‑hosted.
- `training‑data` – Generates or processes training data.
- `agent‑runtime` – Hosts agent execution.
- `protocol` – Defines a communication or interaction protocol.
- `visualization` – Includes visual/demo components.
