## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- `plato` – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export)
- `ensign` – Specialist model adapters (LoRA exports from rooms)
- `holodeck` – Interactive simulation environments (MUD, sentiment NPCs)
- `simulator` – Fleet/data simulation for training generation
- `constraint-theory` – Geometric/relational constraint systems
- `cudaclaw` – GPU-resident agent runtime
- `flux` – Self-assembling bytecode VM
- `shell` – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (crab trap)
- `doctrine` – Protocol definitions (Deadband, P0/P1/P2)
- `tools` – Utilities, APIs, model configs
- `research` – Exploratory code, rabbit trails, experiments
- `archive` – Inactive, deprecated, or historical
- `docs` – Documentation, memory, brand assets

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `rust` / `python` / `cpp` – Primary language
- `training` – Involves model training
- `edge` – Jetson/edge deployment focus
- `cloud` – Cloud/lighthouse services
- `visual` – UI, visualization, demo
- `integration` – Connects multiple systems
- `test` – Testing suite or examples
- `wip` – Work in progress, unstable
