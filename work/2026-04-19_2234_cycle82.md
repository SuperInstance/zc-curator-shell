## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, tile management)
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation environments (MUD server, NPCs)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint solving (snapping, alignment)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime (kernel-level execution)
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM (modular execution)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (crab trap, classification, scoring)
- **ensign** – Specialist model export (LoRA adapters, fine‑tuning pipelines)
- **zeroclaw** – Agent framework (boot camp, role definitions, tile workflows)
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specifications
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, service management
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit trails
- **legacy** – Archived, deprecated, or inactive projects
- **staging** – In‑progress, unintegrated, or prototype work

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `training` – Generates or consumes training data
- `simulation` – Simulated environments or agents
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents/models
- `infrastructure` – Foundational services (rooms, servers, comms)
- `geometry` – Spatial/geometric reasoning
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated or CUDA‑based
- `edge` – Edge‑deployable (Jetson, resource‑constrained)
- `protocol` – Defines communication or interaction patterns
- `visual` – Involves visual output (demos, UI, rendering)
- `archive` – Read‑only, historical reference
