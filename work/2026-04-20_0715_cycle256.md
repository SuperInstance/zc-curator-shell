## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA export, self‑training environments
- **holodeck** – MUD servers, NPC simulation, sentiment‑aware worlds
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solvers
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, kernel‑level execution
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Hermit‑crab capture algorithms, external intelligence ingestion
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping
- **zeroclaw‑bootcamp** – Agent training pipeline, tile‑based skill development

**Support Categories:**
- **docs** – Documentation, RFCs, protocol specifications
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, CLI helpers
- **research** – Experimental notes, rabbit‑trail explorations
- **archive** – Deprecated, inactive, or historical projects
- **asset** – Brand materials, icons, logos, visual identity
- **example** – Demos, tutorials, reference implementations
- **test** – Test suites, benchmarking, validation

**Status Tags:**
- `active` – Actively developed, part of current fleet operations
- `stale` – Low activity, may need revival or archival
- `archive` – No longer maintained, kept for reference
- `experimental` – High‑risk, exploratory, not production‑ready
- `core` – Essential to fleet function, high priority
