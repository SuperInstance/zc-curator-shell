## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet‑knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato‑system** – Training rooms, LoRA export, tile‑based learning
- **simulation‑engine** – Fleet, environment, or game‑world simulation
- **runtime‑engine** – Agent execution, GPU‑resident runtime, bytecode VM
- **constraint‑engine** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, CAD‑like logic
- **shell‑bootstrapping** – Crab‑trap algorithms, external intelligence capture
- **research‑trail** – Exploratory code, experiments, proofs‑of‑concept
- **tool‑infrastructure** – CLI tools, service scripts, health monitors
- **documentation** – READMEs, protocols, memory, brand assets
- **abandoned‑staging** – Inactive, deprecated, or archived projects

**Tags (applied within domains):**
- `training‑data` – Generates or processes training tiles
- `lora‑adapter` – Exports or applies LoRA adapters
- `real‑time` – Requires low‑latency or real‑time execution
- `edge‑deploy` – Targets Jetson or other edge hardware
- `cloud‑service` – Cloud‑hosted, long‑running service
- `rust‑native` – Primary language is Rust
- `python‑glue` – Python‑based integration or scripting
- `experimental` – High‑risk, unstable, or research‑grade
- `stable‑core` – Production‑ready, well‑tested core component
