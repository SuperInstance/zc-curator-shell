## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet‑knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato‑system** – Training rooms, LoRA export, self‑training infrastructure.
- **holodeck‑system** – MUD server, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint‑based AI.
- **cudaclaw‑runtime** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture.
- **agent‑framework** – Agent orchestration, communication, decision‑making.
- **research‑trail** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit‑hole investigations.
- **tool‑utility** – DevOps, monitoring, deployment, helper scripts.
- **documentation** – READMEs, protocols, vision documents, memory.
- **brand‑assets** – Logos, icons, visual identity, marketing materials.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Involves model training or fine‑tuning.
- `simulation` – Simulated environments or agents.
- `rust` – Primary language Rust.
- `python` – Primary language Python.
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch.
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated or GPU‑resident.
- `edge` – Designed for edge deployment (Jetson, etc.).
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native or cloud‑hosted.
- `protocol` – Defines a communication or interaction protocol.
- `archive` – Inactive, deprecated, or historical.
- `merge‑candidate` – Could be merged into another repo.
- `abandoned` – No recent activity, likely dead.
