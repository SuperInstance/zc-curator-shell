## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training systems
- **holodeck** – MUD server, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solving
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation
- **shell‑bootstrapping** – Algorithms to capture external intelligence (classify→score→complicate→capture)
- **mirror‑play** – I2I training between two PLATO sessions, LoRA‑as‑room
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping

**Support Categories:**
- **tools‑scripts** – Utility scripts, service management, automation
- **docs‑research** – Documentation, research notes, protocol definitions
- **experiments** – One‑off tests, prototypes, exploratory code
- **assets** – Brand assets, icons, logos, visual identity
- **learning‑rooms** – Preset training rooms, curriculum, boot‑camp materials

**Status Tags:**
- **active** – Actively maintained, part of current fleet stack
- **archive** – Historical, not actively developed
- **stub** – Minimal placeholder, may be expanded later
- **abandoned** – No recent activity, likely deprecated
