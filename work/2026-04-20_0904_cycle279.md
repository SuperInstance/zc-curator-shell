## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Core Domains:**
- `plato/` – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training systems
- `holodeck/` – MUD server, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation
- `fleet‑simulator/` – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- `constraint‑theory/` – Geometric snapping, constraint‑solving engines
- `cudaclaw/` – GPU‑resident agent runtime, kernel‑level execution
- `flux‑runtime/` – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation

**Support Categories:**
- `shell/` – Bootstrapping, agent capture, classification/scoring pipelines
- `tooling/` – CLI utilities, dev‑ops scripts, service management
- `research/` – Experimental trails, concept proofs, academic notes
- `archive/` – Deprecated, historical, or inactive projects
- `docs/` – Documentation, protocol specs, architecture diagrams
- `assets/` – Brand art, icons, logos, visual identity

**Tags (additional context):**
- `rust` – Implemented in Rust
- `python` – Implemented in Python
- `training` – Generates or consumes training data
- `agent‑runtime` – Hosts or executes agents
- `simulation` – Simulated environments
- `edge` – Targets edge/Jetson devices
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native or lighthouse services
- `wip` – Work in progress, unstable
- `stable` – Production‑ready, tested
