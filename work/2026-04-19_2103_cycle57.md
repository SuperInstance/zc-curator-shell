## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training systems
- **holodeck** – MUD server, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning libraries
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Hermit‑crab‑style capture of external agent intelligence
- **mirror‑play** – I2I (image‑to‑image) training between two PLATO sessions

**Support Categories:**
- **docs** – Documentation, protocol definitions, memory files
- **tools** – CLI utilities, service scripts, health monitors
- **research** – Exploratory code, rabbit‑trail investigations, prototypes
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or merged projects
- **asset** – Brand materials, icons, logos, visual identity

**Tags (used within domains):**
- `rust` | `python` | `cpp` – Primary language
- `training` – Produces or consumes training tiles
- `simulation` – Agent‑environment interaction
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents
- `library` – Reusable code, no standalone service
- `service` – Long‑running process (server, monitor, etc.)
- `cli` – Command‑line tool
- `experimental` – High risk, not production‑ready
