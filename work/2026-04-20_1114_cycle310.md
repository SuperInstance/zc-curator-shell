## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos (plato-torch, holodeck-rust, etc.), I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training algorithms.
- **simulator** – Fleet/agent simulation for generating training data.
- **holodeck** – MUD/server environments with NPCs, sentiment, interaction.
- **constraint** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, CAD‑like logic.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, kernel‑level execution.
- **flux** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, runtime composition.
- **shell** – Bootstrapping, capture‑classify‑score‑complicate algorithms.
- **protocol** – Communication standards, deadband, inter‑agent rules.
- **tool** – Utilities, scripts, CLI tools for fleet operations.
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit‑trail documents.
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or historical projects.
- **meta** – Documentation, brand assets, fleet‑wide coordination.

**Tags (additional context):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data/tiles.
- `edge` – Designed for edge deployment (Jetson, resource‑constrained).
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native, lighthouse, orchestration.
- `rust` / `python` / `cpp` – Primary language.
- `experimental` – High risk, unstable, proof‑of‑concept.
- `stable` – Production‑ready, tested.
- `abandoned` – No recent activity, likely dead.
- `merge‑candidate` – Could be folded into another repo.
