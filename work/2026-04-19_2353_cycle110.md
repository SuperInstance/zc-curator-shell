## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA export, self‑training infrastructure.
- **holodeck** – MUD servers, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solvers.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency inference.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic computation.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Crab‑trap algorithms for capturing external intelligence.
- **mirror‑play** – I2I (image‑to‑image) training loops between PLATO sessions.
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping.
- **ensign** – Lightweight adapters (LoRA) exported from trained rooms.
- **vessel** – Agent‑specific workspaces, local state, personal tools.
- **research** – Exploratory code, rabbit‑trails, concept proofs.
- **tooling** – DevOps, monitoring, deployment, health checks.
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specifications.
- **brand** – Logos, wordmarks, visual identity assets.

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `rust` – Implemented in Rust.
- `python` – Implemented in Python.
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch.
- `cuda` – GPU‑accelerated.
- `edge` – Jetson/edge‑deployable.
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native/lighthouse.
- `training` – Generates or consumes training data.
- `simulation` – Simulates agents/environments.
- `protocol` – Defines a communication or decision protocol.
- `archive` – Historical, not actively maintained.
