## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

### Core Domains
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (plato-torch, plato-ensign)
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation environments (holodeck-rust)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint systems (constraint-theory-core)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (crab-trap)
- **mirror-play** – Iterative LoRA training between two PLATO systems
- **deadband-protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework
- **zeroclaw** – Agent runtime/coordination (zeroclaw-runtime)

### Support Categories
- **docs** – Documentation, specifications, protocol definitions
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, CLI tools
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit trails
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or historical projects
- **asset** – Brand assets, images, design files
- **learning** – Tutorials, examples, boot camp materials

### Status Tags
- **active** – Actively developed, part of current fleet
- **stale** – Not recently updated, but potentially useful
- **abandoned** – No recent activity, likely deprecated
- **experiment** – One-off test or proof of concept
- **template** – Boilerplate or starter project
