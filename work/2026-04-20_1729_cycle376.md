## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training systems.
- **holodeck** – MUD servers, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, constraint‑solving engines.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture.
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping.
- **agent‑runtime** – Agent execution environments, orchestration.
- **tools‑config** – Model APIs, tool configurations, benchmark suites.
- **research‑trail** – Exploratory research, rabbit‑trail documents.
- **docs‑brand** – Documentation, branding assets, identity materials.
- **legacy‑archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or experimental projects.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data.
- `simulation` – Simulates environments or agents.
- `runtime` – Execution environment.
- `geometry` – Spatial/geometric reasoning.
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated.
- `rust` – Written in Rust.
- `python` – Written in Python.
- `typescript` – Written in TypeScript.
- `prototype` – Early‑stage, not production‑ready.
- `tool` – Utility, script, or configuration.
- `research` – Exploratory, non‑operational.
- `archive` – No longer active.
