## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training.
- **holodeck** – MUD server, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, CAD‑like logic.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture.
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, safety‑first navigation.
- **agent‑orchestration** – Multi‑agent coordination, task scheduling, comms.
- **tools‑config** – Model APIs, tool definitions, runtime configuration.
- **docs‑research** – Documentation, whitepapers, research trails, memory.
- **experimental** – Proof‑of‑concept, one‑off tests, sandbox projects.
- **abandoned‑stale** – Inactive, unmaintained, or superseded repos.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data.
- `simulation` – Synthetic environment or agent‑in‑loop.
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents.
- `geometry` – Spatial math, constraints, CAD.
- `protocol` – Communication or decision standard.
- `infrastructure` – Foundational service or platform.
- `research` – Exploratory, non‑production.
- `archive` – Historical, kept for reference.
