## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training algorithms.

- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solving.

- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture.
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, safety‑first navigation.
- **agent‑orchestration** – Multi‑agent coordination, task scheduling, communication.
- **tool‑integration** – API wrappers, model connectors, external service adapters.
- **research‑trail** – Exploratory code, experiments, one‑off proofs‑of‑concept.
- **docs‑brand** – Documentation, branding assets, communication materials.
- **legacy‑archive** – Old prototypes, deprecated code, historical reference.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Involves model training, LoRA, fine‑tuning.
- `simulation` – Synthetic environment, agent‑in‑the‑loop.
- `runtime` – Execution environment, VM, server.
- `geometry` – Spatial math, constraints, snapping.
- `gpu` – CUDA, GPU‑resident, high‑performance compute.
- `protocol` – Communication protocol, decision framework.
- `orchestration` – Multi‑agent coordination.
- `bootstrap` – Bootstrapping, shell algorithms.
- `research` – Experimental, not production‑ready.
- `docs` – Documentation only.
- `archive` – Inactive, historical.
