From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

- **holodeck** – MUD server, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solving
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, kernel‑level execution

**Support Categories:**
- **docs** – Documentation, specifications, protocol definitions
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, CLI helpers
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit‑trail investigations
- **legacy** – Old prototypes, deprecated systems, historical artifacts
- **staging** – In‑progress work, unmerged branches, sandbox environments

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `rust` – Written in Rust
- `python` – Written in Python
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch
- `cuda` – GPU‑accelerated
- `training` – Generates or consumes training data
- `simulation` – Simulates agents or environments
- `protocol` – Defines a communication or decision protocol
- `infrastructure` – Core platform service
