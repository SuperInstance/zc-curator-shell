## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, tile ingestion)
- **holodeck** – Simulation environments (MUD server, NPCs, sentiment)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint solving (snapping, alignment)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime (kernel fusion, low-latency execution)
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM (dynamic reconfiguration)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (classify→score→complicate→capture)
- **mirror-play** – Iterative image-to-image LoRA training (two PLATO sessions)
- **deadband-protocol** – Decision theory (P0/P1/P2 negative space navigation)
- **tools** – Model APIs, benchmarks, utility scripts
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specifications
- **experimental** – Research, rabbit trails, unproven concepts
- **abandoned** – Inactive, deprecated, or superseded projects

**Tags (for cross-cutting concerns):**
- `rust` – Implemented in Rust
- `python` – Implemented in Python
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch
- `training` – Generates or consumes training data
- `simulation` – Simulates agents or environments
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents
- `research` – Exploratory, not yet production
- `infrastructure` – Foundational systems (rooms, servers, etc.)
