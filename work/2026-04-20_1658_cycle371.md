## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA export, self‑training infrastructure.
- **holodeck** – Multi‑user dungeon (MUD) servers, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Fleet‑level simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, constraint‑solving engines.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency inference.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, modular execution.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture (crab‑trap).
- **deadband‑protocol** – Implementation of P0/P1/P2 decision protocol.
- **agent‑framework** – Agent orchestration, communication, task management.
- **tools‑config** – Model APIs, tool configurations, benchmarking suites.
- **docs‑knowledge** – Documentation, memory, training materials, brand assets.
- **experimental** – Research, prototypes, rabbit‑trail explorations.
- **abandoned‑stale** – Inactive, unmaintained, or deprecated projects.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data.
- `simulation` – Simulated environments or agents.
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents/models.
- `geometry` – Spatial reasoning, constraint solving.
- `edge` – Optimized for edge/Jetson deployment.
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native, lighthouse services.
- `rust` – Primary language Rust.
- `python` – Primary language Python.
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch.
- `lora` – LoRA training or adapter management.
- `mud` – Multi‑user dungeon/dialog system.
- `protocol` – Communication or decision protocols.
- `archive` – Historical, not actively developed.
