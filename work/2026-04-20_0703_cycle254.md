## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and existing categorized repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Primary Categories:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA export, self‑training environments.
- **holodeck** – Multi‑user dungeons, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulations.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Fleet‑wide simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint‑based AI.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, modular execution.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms for capturing external intelligence.
- **deadband‑protocol** – Implementation of the P0/P1/P2 decision framework.
- **docs‑research** – Research notes, literature reviews, speculative design.
- **tools‑scripts** – Utility scripts, service guards, deployment helpers.
- **experimental** – Proof‑of‑concept, one‑off tests, sandbox projects.
- **abandoned‑stale** – Inactive, unmaintained, or superseded repos.

**Tags (additional context):**
- `training‑data` – Generates or processes training tiles.
- `agent‑runtime` – Hosts or executes agent logic.
- `simulation` – Simulates environments or agents.
- `geometry` – Handles spatial/mathematical constraints.
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated components.
- `rust` / `python` / `cpp` – Primary language.
- `wip` – Work in progress, unstable.
- `archive` – Historical, kept for reference.
