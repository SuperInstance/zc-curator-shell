## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, tile handling)
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation environments (MUD, NPCs, sentiment)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint solving (snapping, alignment)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime (kernel fusion, low-latency execution)
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM (dynamic reconfiguration)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (classify→score→complicate→capture)
- **ensign** – Specialist model exports (LoRA adapters, fine‑tuned weights)
- **deadband** – Protocol implementation (P0/P1/P2 decision layers)
- **oracle** – Cloud lighthouse services (long‑running, coordination, memory)
- **edge** – Jetson/embedded deployment (bare‑metal, resource‑constrained)
- **tools** – Development utilities, CLI tools, API wrappers
- **research** – Experimental code, rabbit‑trails, concept proofs
- **docs** – Documentation, specifications, architecture notes
- **brand** – Visual identity, logos, wordmarks, brand assets

**Status Tags:**
- **active** – Actively developed, part of current fleet operations
- **archive** – Historical, not actively developed but preserved
- **stub** – Skeleton or placeholder, minimal content
- **abandoned** – Incomplete, orphaned, likely not to be resumed
- **merge-candidate** – Could be merged into another repo (duplicate/overlap)
