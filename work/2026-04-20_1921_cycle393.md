## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training algorithms
- **holodeck** – MUD server, NPC simulation, sentiment‑aware environments
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solving
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic code generation
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture
- **mirror‑play** – I2I (image‑to‑image) training loops, dual‑PLATO sessions
- **deadband‑protocol** – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping

**Support Categories:**
- **tooling** – CLI utilities, dev scripts, service management
- **docs** – Documentation, specifications, protocol descriptions
- **research** – Experimental code, rabbit‑trail explorations, concept proofs
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or historical projects
- **asset** – Brand materials, logos, icons, visual identity

**Lifecycle Tags:**
- `active` – Actively developed, part of current fleet operations
- `stale` – No recent commits, but potentially reusable
- `abandoned` – No commits >1 year, unmaintained
- `experimental` – Proof‑of‑concept, not production‑ready
- `core` – Essential to fleet architecture
