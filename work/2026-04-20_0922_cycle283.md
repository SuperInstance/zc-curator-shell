## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, tile handling)
- **holodeck** – Simulation environments (MUD server, NPCs, sentiment-aware worlds)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint solving (snapping, alignment)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime (kernel-level execution)
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM (modular execution)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (crab trap, classification, scoring)
- **ensign** – Specialist model exports (LoRA adapters, fine‑tuned models)
- **tooling** – Development utilities (CLIs, monitors, API wrappers)
- **research** – Experimental code, rabbit‑trail explorations, prototypes
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specifications
- **brand** – Visual identity, logos, wordmarks, brand assets

**Tags (additional context):**
- `training` – Used for model training or generates training data
- `simulation` – Simulates environments or agents
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents/models
- `infrastructure` – Foundational systems (servers, APIs, orchestration)
- `experimental` – High‑risk, early‑stage, not production‑ready
- `archive` – Inactive, deprecated, or historical reference
- `integration` – Connects multiple systems or protocols
