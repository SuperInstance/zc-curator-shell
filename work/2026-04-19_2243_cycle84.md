## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (plato-torch, plato-ensign)
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation environments (holodeck-rust)
- **simulator** – Fleet/agent simulation (fleet-simulator)
- **constraint** – Geometric/relational reasoning (constraint-theory-core)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime
- **flux** – Self-assembling bytecode VM (flux-runtime)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (the Shell concept)
- **mirror-play** – Iterative LoRA training via dual PLATO sessions

**Support Categories:**
- **tools** – Development utilities, scripts, CLI tools
- **docs** – Documentation, specifications, memory
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit trails
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or historical projects
- **asset** – Brand assets, images, design files
- **learning** – Tutorials, examples, boot camp materials

**Status Tags:**
- `active` – Actively developed, part of current fleet
- `stale` – Not recently updated, but potentially useful
- `abandoned` – No activity, likely deprecated
- `experiment` – Proof-of-concept, not production-ready
- `tool` – Utility supporting active projects
