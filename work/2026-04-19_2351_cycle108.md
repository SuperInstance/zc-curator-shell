## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Core Domains:**
- `plato` – Training room infrastructure, LoRA export, self‑training environments
- `holodeck` – Multi‑user dungeon (MUD) servers, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation
- `fleet‑simulator` – Fleet‑level simulation for generating training data
- `constraint‑theory` – Geometric snapping, constraint solving, spatial reasoning
- `cudaclaw` – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution
- `flux‑runtime` – Self‑assembling bytecode virtual machine
- `shell‑bootstrap` – Bootstrapping algorithms for capturing external intelligence
- `mirror‑play` – Iterative screen‑to‑screen training (I2I), LoRA‑as‑room
- `deadband‑protocol` – P0/P1/P2 decision framework, negative‑space mapping

**Support Categories:**
- `docs` – Documentation, specifications, protocol definitions
- `tools` – Utilities, scripts, service management
- `research` – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit‑trail investigations
- `archive` – Inactive, deprecated, or historical projects
- `asset` – Brand materials, logos, icons, visual identity
- `training‑data` – Datasets, tile collections, experience logs

**Status Tags:**
- `active` – Actively developed, part of current fleet operations
- `stale` – No recent commits, but potentially reusable
- `abandoned` – No commits >1 year, unmaintained
- `experimental` – Proof‑of‑concept, not production‑ready
- `core‑infra` – Essential to fleet function (e.g., plato‑torch, holodeck‑rust)
