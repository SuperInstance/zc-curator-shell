## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Categories:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA trainers, self‑play environments
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation, MUD servers, NPC systems
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation, training data generation
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric/relational constraint solving, snapping engines
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, kernel‑level execution
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, dynamic compilation
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture
- **docs‑research** – Documentation, research notes, rabbit‑trail explorations
- **tool‑utility** – Development tools, scripts, CLI utilities, health monitors
- **experimental** – Proof‑of‑concept, one‑off tests, sandbox projects
- **archive‑abandoned** – Inactive, deprecated, or superseded work

**Tags (additional context):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data/tiles
- `rust` / `python` / `cpp` – Primary language
- `edge` – Jetson/embedded focus
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native, scalable services
- `visual` – Graphics, UI, visualization
- `protocol` – Communication standards, APIs
- `mirror‑play` – Implements I2I mirror‑play pattern
