## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (plato-torch, plato-ensign)
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation environments (holodeck-rust)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint systems (constraint-theory-core)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (the "crab trap")
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specs
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, service management
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit trails
- **legacy** – Old prototypes, deprecated systems
- **external** – Forks of third-party projects

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Involves model training or LoRA export
- `simulation` – Simulated environments or agents
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents
- `geometry` – Spatial/constraint solving
- `infrastructure` – Core platform services
- `agent` – Agent-specific logic or frameworks
- `monitoring` – Health checks, logging, observability
- `archive` – Historical, not actively developed
