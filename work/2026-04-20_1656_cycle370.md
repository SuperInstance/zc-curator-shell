## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos (plato-torch, holodeck-rust, fleet-simulator, constraint-theory-core, cudaclaw, flux-runtime), I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- `plato` – Training rooms, LoRA export, self‑training infrastructure
- `simulation` – Fleet, agent, or environment simulators for training data
- `runtime` – Agent execution environments (GPU‑resident, VM, bytecode)
- `constraint` – Geometric, logical, or behavioral constraint systems
- `holodeck` – Interactive narrative/MUD servers with NPCs
- `shell` – Bootstrapping, capture, and classification algorithms
- `research` – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit‑trails
- `tool` – Utilities, CLI tools, service scripts
- `docs` – Documentation, memory, protocol specifications
- `brand` – Logos, wordmarks, visual identity assets
- `archive` – Inactive, deprecated, or merged repositories

**Tags (additional context):**
- `rust` / `python` / `cpp` – Primary language
- `training` – Generates or uses training data
- `edge` – Targets edge devices (Jetson)
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native or lighthouse services
- `lora` – Involves LoRA adapters
- `agent` – Agent‑framework related
- `mirror-play` – Implements mirror‑play I2I
- `deadband` – Implements deadband protocol
