## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA export, self‑training infrastructure.
- **holodeck** – MUD servers, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint solvers.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, modular execution.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external intelligence capture.
- **docs‑brand** – Documentation, branding assets, identity materials.
- **tools‑scripts** – Utility scripts, service guards, deployment helpers.
- **research** – Rabbit‑trail explorations, concept prototypes, academic notes.
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or merged projects.
- **experimental** – Early‑stage, high‑risk, unproven experiments.

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `rust` – Implemented in Rust.
- `python` – Implemented in Python.
- `training` – Generates or consumes training data.
- `simulation` – Simulates environments or agents.
- `edge` – Targets edge devices (Jetson, etc.).
- `cloud` – Cloud‑native or cloud‑hosted.
- `lora` – Involves LoRA adapters or fine‑tuning.
- `mud` – Multi‑user dungeon / text‑based world.
- `protocol` – Defines a communication or interaction protocol.
- `visual` – Includes visual assets or UI components.
