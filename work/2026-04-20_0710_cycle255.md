## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Core Domains:**
- `plato` – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, self‑training)
- `holodeck` – Interactive simulation environments (MUD, NPCs, sentiment)
- `fleet-simulator` – Multi‑agent simulation for training data generation
- `constraint-theory` – Geometric/relational constraint solving
- `cudaclaw` – GPU‑resident agent runtime
- `flux-runtime` – Self‑assembling bytecode VM
- `shell` – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (crab trap)
- `ensign` – LoRA adapters exported from room experience
- `zeroclaw` – DeepSeek‑chat agent with git‑repo‑as‑shell
- `deadband` – Protocol implementation (P0/P1/P2)

**Support Categories:**
- `docs` – Documentation, memory, protocol definitions
- `tools` – Utilities, scripts, service management
- `research` – Exploratory code, rabbit‑trail investigations
- `archive` – Inactive, deprecated, or experimental repos
- `asset` – Brand materials, logos, icons
- `test` – Test suites, benchmarks, validation

**Status Tags:**
- `active` – Actively maintained, part of current fleet
- `stale` – Not recently updated but potentially useful
- `abandoned` – No commits >1 year, likely dead
- `experimental` – Proof‑of‑concept, not production‑ready
- `merge-candidate` – Could be consolidated into another repo
