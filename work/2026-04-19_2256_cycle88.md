## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, tile handling)
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation environments (MUD, NPCs, sentiment)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint solving (snapping, alignment)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime (low-latency execution)
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM (modular execution)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (crab trap, classification)
- **ensign** – Specialist model export (LoRA adapters, fine‑tuning)
- **deadband** – Protocol implementation (P0/P1/P2 decision layers)
- **oracle** – Cloud lighthouse services (orchestration, memory)
- **edge** – Jetson/embedded deployment (bare‑metal, resource‑constrained)
- **tooling** – Development utilities (CLIs, scripts, health checks)
- **research** – Experimental concepts, rabbit‑trail explorations
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, brand assets
- **legacy** – Archived, deprecated, or inactive prototypes

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `rust` | `python` | `cpp` – Primary language
- `training` – Generates or consumes training data
- `simulation` – Simulated environment
- `agent` – Multi‑agent system component
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated
- `embedded` – Runs on edge hardware
- `protocol` – Communication or decision protocol
- `visual` – Involves graphics/UI
- `cli` – Command‑line tool
- `archive` – Inactive but kept for reference
