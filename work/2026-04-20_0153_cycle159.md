## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet‑knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato‑torch** – Training room presets, self‑training infrastructure.
- **plato‑ensign** – LoRA export from room experience.
- **holodeck‑rust** – MUD server with sentiment‑aware NPCs.
- **fleet‑simulator** – Multi‑agent simulation for training data.
- **constraint‑theory‑core** – Geometric snapping, constraint solving.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime.
- **flux‑runtime** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM.
- **shell‑bootstrap** – Crab‑trap algorithms for capturing external intelligence.

**Support Categories:**
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol definitions.
- **tools** – CLI utilities, service scripts, health monitors.
- **research** – Rabbit‑trail explorations, concept prototypes.
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or merged repos.
- **experimental** – Early‑stage, unproven, high‑risk ideas.

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training tiles.
- `simulation` – Involves simulated environments or agents.
- `rust` – Primary language is Rust.
- `python` – Primary language is Python.
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated or CUDA‑based.
- `edge` – Designed for edge deployment (Jetson, etc.).
- `protocol` – Defines a communication or interaction protocol.
- `visual` – Involves graphics, UI, or visualization.
