## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading the active fleet repos (`plato-torch`, `plato-ensign`, `holodeck-rust`, `fleet-simulator`, `constraint-theory-core`, `cudaclaw`, `flux-runtime`), I derive the following primary categories:

- **plato** – PLATO room server, training, LoRA export infrastructure.
- **simulator** – Fleet, agent, or environment simulation for training data.
- **holodeck** – MUD server, NPC, sentiment-aware interactive environments.
- **constraint** – Geometric, spatial, or logical constraint solving.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime, low-level CUDA/GPU integration.
- **flux** – VM, bytecode, self-assembling runtime systems.
- **shell** – Bootstrapping, capture, classification, scoring algorithms (the "crab trap").
- **mirror-play** – I2I (image-to-image) training, dual-PLATO session data generation.
- **deadband** – Protocol implementation (P0/P1/P2), safety channels.
- **tool** – CLI utilities, scripts, service management.
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specs, brand assets.
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit trails.
- **legacy** – Old prototypes, deprecated, or inactive code.
- **misc** – Unclassified, personal, or one-off projects.
