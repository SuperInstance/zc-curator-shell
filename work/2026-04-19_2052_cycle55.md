## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos (plato-torch, holodeck-rust, fleet-simulator, constraint-theory-core, cudaclaw, flux-runtime), I derive the following taxonomy:

**Core Categories:**
- **plato** – Training room systems, LoRA export, self‑training environments.
- **holodeck** – Multi‑user dungeons, sentiment‑aware NPCs, interactive simulation.
- **fleet‑sim** – Fleet‑level simulation for generating training data.
- **constraint‑theory** – Geometric snapping, spatial reasoning, constraint‑solving engines.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency inference.
- **flux** – Self‑assembling bytecode VM, runtime composition.
- **shell‑boot** – Bootstrapping algorithms, external‑agent capture (crab‑trap).
- **docs‑spec** – Documentation, specifications, protocol definitions.
- **tools‑scripts** – Utility scripts, service guards, deployment helpers.
- **research** – Experimental ideas, rabbit‑trail explorations, concept proofs.
- **abandoned** – Inactive, deprecated, or archived projects.
- **external‑fork** – Forks of third‑party projects used as dependencies.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Involves model training or fine‑tuning.
- `inference` – Model serving, runtime execution.
- `simulation` – Simulated environments, synthetic data generation.
- `geometry` – Spatial math, CAD‑like operations.
- `rust` – Primary language is Rust.
- `python` – Primary language is Python.
- `cuda` – GPU‑accelerated CUDA code.
- `api` – Provides an API or service interface.
- `cli` – Command‑line tool.
- `web` – Web interface or server.
- `experimental` – Early stage, unstable.
- `stable` – Production‑ready, tested.
