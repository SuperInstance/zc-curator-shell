## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (presets, LoRA export, tile handling)
- **holodeck** – Simulation environments (MUD server, NPCs, sentiment)
- **fleet-simulator** – Multi-agent simulation for training data generation
- **constraint-theory** – Geometric/relational constraint solving (snapping, alignment)
- **cudaclaw** – GPU-resident agent runtime (kernel-level execution)
- **flux-runtime** – Self-assembling bytecode VM (modular execution)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (crab trap, classification)
- **ensign** – Specialist model exports (LoRA adapters, fine‑tuning)
- **vessel** – Agent containers/orchestration (Docker, service management)
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specifications
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, CLI tools for fleet operations
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit trails
- **legacy** – Archived, deprecated, or inactive projects
- **stub** – Minimal placeholder repos (README only, no code)

**Tags (for cross‑cutting concerns):**
- `rust` | `python` | `cpp` – Primary language
- `training` – Generates or processes training data
- `simulation` – Simulates agents/environments
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents
- `geometry` – Spatial/geometric operations
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated or CUDA‑based
- `edge` – Designed for edge deployment (Jetson, etc.)
- `protocol` – Communication or interaction protocols
- `archive` – Historical, not actively maintained
