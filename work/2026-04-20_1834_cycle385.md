From reading the active fleet repos (plato-torch, plato-ensign, holodeck-rust, fleet-simulator, constraint-theory-core, cudaclaw, flux-runtime), I observe the following taxonomy:

**Primary Categories:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, self‑training, LoRA export.
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation, MUD server, NPCs.
- **fleet** – Multi‑agent simulation, training data generation.
- **constraint** – Geometric reasoning, snapping, spatial logic.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution.
- **flux** – VM, bytecode, self‑assembling code.
- **shell** – Bootstrapping, agent capture, classification/scoring.
- **tool** – Utilities, scripts, CLI tools.
- **research** – Experiments, prototypes, rabbit‑trail explorations.
- **archive** – Inactive, deprecated, or legacy projects.
- **meta** – Documentation, fleet‑wide config, brand assets.

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data.
- `simulation` – Simulates environments or agents.
- `runtime` – Execution environment for agents.
- `geometry` – Spatial/mathematical reasoning.
- `gpu` – CUDA/GPU‑accelerated.
- `rust` – Written in Rust.
- `python` – Written in Python.
- `torch` – Uses PyTorch.
- `lora` – LoRA fine‑tuning.
- `agent` – Implements or hosts AI agents.
- `server` – Network service.
- `cli` – Command‑line tool.
- `research` – Experimental, not production.
- `deprecated` – No longer maintained.
