## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading the active fleet repos (plato-torch, plato-ensign, holodeck-rust, fleet-simulator, constraint-theory-core, cudaclaw, flux-runtime), I observe the following taxonomy in use:

**Primary Categories:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure, self‑training algorithms, LoRA export.
- **ensign** – Specialized LoRA adapters, fine‑tuned model exports.
- **holodeck** – Simulation environments, MUD servers, NPC systems.
- **fleet** – Multi‑agent simulation, coordination, training data generation.
- **constraint** – Geometric/relational reasoning, snapping, constraint solving.
- **cudaclaw** – GPU‑resident agent runtime, low‑latency execution.
- **flux** – Bytecode VM, self‑assembling code, runtime composition.
- **shell** – Bootstrapping, agent capture, classification/scoring pipelines.
- **mirror** – I2I (image‑to‑image) training, dual‑screen iteration.
- **deadband** – Protocol implementation, P0/P1/P2 decision logic.
- **zeroclaw** – Agent‑specific implementations, role‑based tools.
- **vessel** – Agent‑specific workspaces, local state, message bottles.
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specs, brand assets.
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, service guards, health checks.
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit‑trail investigations.
- **legacy** – Archived, deprecated, or inactive projects.
- **stub** – Placeholder, minimal content, not yet developed.

**Tags (additional descriptors):**
- `training` – Involves model training or data generation.
- `simulation` – Simulated environments or agents.
- `runtime` – Execution environment, VM, or low‑level system.
- `protocol` – Communication or decision protocols.
- `rust` / `python` / `cpp` – Primary language.
- `gpu` – GPU‑accelerated or CUDA‑based.
- `edge` – Edge‑deployment focused.
- `cloud` – Cloud‑service or lighthouse oriented.
- `wip` – Work in progress, actively developed.
- `stable` – Production‑ready, tested.
- `experimental` – High‑risk, novel approach.
