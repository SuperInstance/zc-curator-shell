## 1. Fleet Taxonomy (Derived from Active Fleet Repos)

From reading fleet-knowledge/ and active fleet repos, I derive this working taxonomy:

**Core Domains:**
- **plato** – Training room infrastructure (plato-torch, plato-ensign)
- **holodeck** – Interactive simulation environments (holodeck-rust)
- **simulator** – Fleet/data simulation (fleet-simulator)
- **constraint** – Geometric/rule-based systems (constraint-theory-core)
- **runtime** – Agent execution environments (cudaclaw, flux-runtime)
- **shell** – Bootstrapping/capture algorithms (the-shell)
- **docs** – Documentation, memory, protocol specs
- **tools** – Utilities, scripts, service management
- **research** – Exploratory code, experiments, rabbit trails
- **legacy** – Old prototypes, deprecated systems
- **archive** – Inactive but preserved knowledge

**Tags (for finer granularity):**
- `training` – Produces or consumes training data
- `lora` – LoRA adapter generation/management
- `mud` – Multi‑user dungeon/server
- `gpu` – GPU‑resident execution
- `rust` – Rust codebase
- `python` – Python codebase
- `edge` – Edge‑deployable
- `cloud` – Cloud‑hosted
- `agent` – Agent‑runtime related
- `visual` – Visual/UI components
- `cli` – Command‑line tool
- `service` – Long‑running service
